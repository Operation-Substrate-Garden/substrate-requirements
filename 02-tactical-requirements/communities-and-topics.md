# Communities and Topics

Communities and Topics are the primary structures for organizing collaboration within Operation Substrate Garden.

## 1. Communities: Organic Collaboration Clusters
- **Definition:** Communities are self-organizing groups of users focused on specific domains (e.g., "AI and Defense," "Cybersecurity Strategy").
- **Creation:** Any verified user from a whitelisted organization can propose the creation of a new community.
- **Membership:** Users join communities to gain access to their specialized topics.
- **Visibility:** Each community can set its visibility to public (within the platform), private (discoverable but requires approval), or hidden (requires an invitation).

## 2. Topics: Focused Professional Dialogue
- **Definition:** Within each community, communication is organized into Topics—specific areas of inquiry or discussion.
- **Organization:** Topics are flat and non-hierarchical within a community, preventing information from being buried in deep subfolders.
- **Content:** Topics can contain threaded discussions, documents, shared links, and interactive polls.
- **Moderation:** Community leads manage topics, ensuring the discussion remains focused and professional.

## 3. Interaction Design
- **Tonal Layering:** The UI for communities and topics must use different shades of the background color to create depth, rather than using thin borders.
- **Typography:** Display titles for communities and topics use the **Manrope** font for a professional yet accessible look.
- **Utility Text:** Descriptions and discussion threads use the **Inter** font for maximum legibility and utility.
